Bet Sizing Guide
Tournament-specific preflop sizing rules — effective stacks matter most
Opening Raise
Standard Open Size
2.0x – 2.5x BB
In tournaments, smaller opens are standard vs cash game. Preserves chips, keeps SPR manageable. Use 2.2x in most spots. Go slightly larger (2.5x) from early positions to charge limpers and widen your fold equity.
100BB: raise to 220 BTN: 2.0x UTG: 2.5x
3-Bet Sizing
Re-raise Sizing
3x – 3.5x in position
4x out of position
Size up when OOP (SB/BB) to compensate for positional disadvantage. Smaller IP because you have postflop leverage. Both ranges should include value + bluffs at correct ratio.
IP: ~3x the open OOP: ~4x the open BB vs BTN: 10-11BB
Squeeze Play
Open + Caller(s) → Squeeze
4x open + 1BB per caller
Each caller widens your sizing. A squeeze needs more fold equity to be profitable. The formula: 4x the original open + 1BB per limper/caller. Callers are trapped between you and the original raiser.
Open 2.5, 1 caller → ~12BB Open 2.5, 2 callers → ~14BB
Shove / All-In
Push or Fold Threshold
≤ 15 BB: consider shoving
Below ~15BB, the math shifts. Shoving removes postflop complexity and maximizes fold equity. Use ICM-adjusted Nash ranges near the bubble. At 10BB, your range widens significantly.
15BB: A8o+, KTs+, 55+ 10BB: any A, K9o+, 44+ 7BB: very wide shove
Calling a 3-Bet
When to Call vs 4-Bet
Call: implied odds hands
4-Bet: polarized range
Suited connectors and small pairs play better as calls (set/straight mining). Big aces (AK, AQ) and TT+ should often go into your 4-bet range. Don't flat 3-bets OOP without strong reason.
Call IP: 78s, 56s, 99 4-Bet: AA, KK, AKs (value) 4-Bet bluff: A5s, A4s
Big Blind Defense
BB vs Open — Sizing Impact
Good pot odds = wide defense
BB gets a discount. Against a 2.5x open you need ~28% equity to break even. This means you can defend much wider from the BB. Against BTN opens, defend ~40-50% of hands. Tighten vs EP.
vs 2x BTN: defend ~50% vs 3x BTN: defend ~35% vs UTG 2.5x: ~30%
Stack-to-Pot Ratio Cheat Sheet

Deep (60BB+)

Standard raises 2.2–2.5x. 3-bets common. Cold-calling ranges widen. Implied odds matter. SPR high — drawing hands gain value.

Mid (30–60BB)

Most tournament spots. Standard open/3-bet structure. ICM starts mattering near bubble. Avoid marginal cold-calls OOP.

Short (15–30BB)

Raise/fold or shove. 3-bets are usually commits. Adjust to ICM pressure. Stealing antes becomes critical. Widen late position opens.

Critical (≤15BB)

Push/fold mode. Use Nash equilibrium ranges. Account for ICM on bubble. Shove >70% of your range from BTN/SB with 10BB.

Core Concepts
The theory behind preflop tournament decisions
🎯
Position is Everything
Acting last postflop is a massive edge. Your preflop range should be built around your positional advantage. LP players open wider because they'll have information on every street.

📐
Range Construction
Ranges aren't random — they're built around equity + playability + blockers. Every range has:

Value hands — Hands you want called (AA, KK, AKs)
Semi-bluffs — Hands with equity + fold equity (A5s, 76s)
Blockers — Hands that reduce villain's strong combos (A5o as a 3-bet bluff blocks AK, AA)

A balanced preflop range makes you unexploitable. If you only 3-bet AA/KK, good players will fold and only call with hands that dominate you rarely.
💰
ICM in Tournaments
ICM (Independent Chip Model) means chips aren't linear in value. Going from 10K → 20K chips doesn't double your equity because busting = $0.

Near the bubble: tighten up significantly
Big stack: apply max pressure on short stacks
Short stack: widen shove range — you need chips

ICM punishes marginal all-in spots. Avoid flipping for your tournament life unless the spot is heavily +EV.
🔢
Pot Odds & MDF
Pot Odds: If facing a bet, calculate: call / (pot + call). This is your required equity to break even.

MDF (Minimum Defense Frequency): How often you must defend to stop your opponent from profiting with any-two-cards bluffs:
MDF = pot / (pot + bet)

Example: Villain 3-bets to 9BB into a 3BB pot → you need 33% equity to call. If you fold more than 25% of hands, they profit with pure bluffs.
⚖️
3-Bet Polarization
A GTO 3-bet range is polarized — it contains very strong hands (value) and some weak hands (bluffs), but skips mediocre hands.

Value: AA, KK, QQ, AKs
Bluffs: A2s–A5s, K5s (blockers)
Call: JJ, TT, AQo, KQs (IP)

Why? Mediocre hands like QJo don't play well in a 3-bet pot. It's better to flat IP or fold OOP.
📊
Antes & Steal Frequency
Once antes are in play, the pot before any action grows significantly. This means stealing the blinds has higher EV and you should open wider from LP.

With 10BB effective stacks and antes:
BTN shove range: ~65–70% of hands
SB shove range: ~55–60% of hands

Antes also mean BB gets better odds to defend — they're already invested. Don't over-steal a BB who knows to defend wide.
